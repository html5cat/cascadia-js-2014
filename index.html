Map Projections

How do you flatten the Earth to show in 2D?

  • Surfaces (plane, cylinder, cone…)
  • Preservation (angle, area, distance…)


Thanks to Mike Bostock and Jason Davies for D3 Geo Projections

Azimuthal (Plane)

Flatten map on a plane, distoration happens at the edges

Cylindrical

Flatten map on a cylinder, distortion happens at the top/bottom

Conic

Flatten map on a cone, distortion happens away from the center

Sterographic (Preserve angle)

Mercator (Preserve meridians)

Equal-Area (Preserve area)

Shapes are always presented with areas proporational to each other.

Equidistant (Preserve distance)

Distances along meridians are preserved.

Gnomonic (Preserve shortest route)

The shortest distance between 2 points is always a straight line

Compromise (Preserve "feeling")

Ignore trying to preserve a metric and make the end result "feel" right

Geographic Coordinate System

Plot data in Latitude/Longitude onto a spherical model of the Earth

Parts of a Geographic Coordinate System

  • Unit of measurement (degrees, minutes, seconds…)
  • Prime meridian (where is 0 longitude)
  • A spheroid (estimate of the size and shape of the earth)
  • A datum (control points used to define lat/lng)

Projected Coordinate System

Plot data in X/Y pairs onto a flat surface.

X=Longitude, Y=Latitude

Say hello to EPSG:3857

PROJCS["WGS 84 / Pseudo-Mercator",
    GEOGCS["WGS 84",
        DATUM["WGS_1984",
            SPHEROID["WGS 84",6378137,298.257223563,
                AUTHORITY["EPSG","7030"]],
            AUTHORITY["EPSG","6326"]],
        PRIMEM["Greenwich",0,
            AUTHORITY["EPSG","8901"]],
        UNIT["degree",0.0174532925199433,
            AUTHORITY["EPSG","9122"]],
        AUTHORITY["EPSG","4326"]],
    PROJECTION["Mercator_1SP"],
    PARAMETER["central_meridian",0],
    PARAMETER["scale_factor",1],
    PARAMETER["false_easting",0],
    PARAMETER["false_northing",0],
    UNIT["metre",1,
        AUTHORITY["EPSG","9001"]],
    AXIS["X",EAST],
    AXIS["Y",NORTH],
    EXTENSION["PROJ4","+proj=merc +a=6378137 +b=6378137 +lat_ts=0.0 +lon_0=0.0 +x_0=0.0 +y_0=0 +k=1.0 +units=m +nadgrids=@null +wktext  +no_defs"],
    AUTHORITY["EPSG","3857"]]

Dealing with Spatial References

  • ogr2ogr

Shapefile to GeoJSON - ogr2ogr

Shapefiles package useful for distributing spatial data.

ogr2ogr parks.geojson PortlandParksTrails -F GeoJSON -t_srs EPSG:4326 -lco COORDINATE_PRECISION=6

CSV to GeoJSON - ogr2ogr

Transform point data in a CSV file to GeoJSON.

ogr2ogr graffiti.geojson graffiti.vrt -f GeoJSON -t_srs EPSG:4326 -lco COORDINATE_PRECISION=-4

CSV to GeoJSON - proj4js

Use proj4js and Node to convert the same CSV
